DIABETIC RETINOPATHY

Definition
Diabetic retinopathy is an eye disease caused by diabetes that damages the blood vessels in the retina. If left untreated, it can lead to vision loss and blindness.

Symptoms
- Blurred vision
- Floaters or dark spots
- Difficulty seeing at night
- Fluctuating vision
- Dark or empty areas in the field of vision
- Vision loss in advanced stages

Causes
Diabetic retinopathy occurs due to prolonged high blood sugar levels, which damage the tiny blood vessels of the retina.

Risk Factors
- Type 1 or Type 2 diabetes
- Poor blood sugar control
- High blood pressure
- High cholesterol
- Long duration of diabetes
- Smoking
- Pregnancy in diabetic patients

Diagnosis
- Dilated eye examination
- Fundus photography
- Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT)
- Fluorescein angiography
- Visual acuity test

Treatment
- Blood sugar control
- Blood pressure management
- Laser photocoagulation
- Anti-VEGF injections
- Steroid injections
- Vitrectomy surgery for severe cases

Prevention
- Regular eye examinations
- Maintain healthy blood sugar levels
- Control blood pressure and cholesterol
- Exercise regularly
- Eat a healthy diet
- Stop smoking

Lifestyle Recommendations
- Eat green leafy vegetables and fruits.
- Maintain a healthy weight.
- Monitor blood glucose regularly.
- Follow prescribed medications.
- Attend annual eye check-ups.
- Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption.

When to Consult a Doctor
Consult an ophthalmologist immediately if you notice sudden vision changes, blurred vision, floaters, or loss of vision.
